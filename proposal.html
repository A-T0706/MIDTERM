Final Redesign Proposal

A calmer and greener public plaza built around comfort, identity, and everyday use.

Proposal Summary

The redesign introduces layered greenery, flexible seating, shaded rest areas, and a more intentional circulation pattern. These changes aim to improve both environmental comfort and social use.

The goal is not just beautification. The redesign supports a plaza that feels healthier, more welcoming, and more connected to the surrounding community.

Main Interventions

  • Tree planters and planting beds
  • Shaded seating clusters
  • Flexible benches and movable chairs
  • Stronger visual framing of pathways
  • Spaces for pause, gathering, and observation

Why More Greenery Matters

More planting can soften the plaza visually, increase comfort, and make the site feel more inviting. It also helps create a stronger sense of place.

In the redesign images, greenery is used both as an environmental tool and as a design language that makes the plaza feel more cared for.
