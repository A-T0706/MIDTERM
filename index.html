Reimagining Harlem Plaza
as a stronger public space for the community

This project studies the current plaza and proposes improvements through AI-assisted redesign, environmental thinking, and public-space planning. The main goals are to add greenery, increase comfort, improve seating, and make the plaza feel more active and inviting.

🌿

More Greenery

New trees, planted edges, and landscape zones would soften the plaza, reduce heat, and create a more welcoming identity.

🪑

Better Seating

Flexible benches and social seating clusters would support studying, resting, and small community gatherings.

☀️

Shade + Comfort

Shade structures and planting can make the plaza more usable throughout the day, especially in warmer weather.

🚶

Safer Movement

Clearer pathways and better spatial organization can improve circulation and make the site easier to navigate.

Current Conditions

The existing plaza already has value as a public space, but parts of it feel exposed, hard, and underused. Research focused on comfort, use, circulation, and visual atmosphere.

Redesign Vision

The proposal introduces more planting, seating, shade, and stronger visual identity so the plaza feels more useful, calm, and community-centered.
