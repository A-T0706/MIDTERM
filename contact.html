Final Reflection
on the Harlem Plaza redesign

This project explored how design can improve a public space without removing its identity. Through site research, diagrams, and AI-assisted image generation, I tested ways to make Harlem Plaza more comfortable, greener, and more welcoming.

🔍

Research

The project began by analyzing the current site and identifying key issues in comfort, use, visibility, and public atmosphere.

🤖

AI Exploration

AI-generated concepts made it possible to test multiple design directions and compare how different changes might affect the plaza.

🎨

Design Thinking

The final design focused on adding greenery, better seating, improved circulation, and a stronger visual identity.

🏙️

Final Outcome

The redesign presents Harlem Plaza as a more welcoming and useful public space for everyday community life.

What I Learned

I learned how research, visual communication, and iterative design can work together to present a stronger proposal.

Project Impact

Even small public-space changes can affect comfort, social use, and the identity of a neighborhood space.
